Norse mythology is a rich and complex belief system that originated with the ancient Scandinavian and Germanic peoples, weaving together tales of mighty gods, fearsome giants, and epic battles. At its core, it tells of the cosmos being divided into nine realms, all connected by the great world tree, Yggdrasil. Odin, the Allfather, is the wise and relentless seeker of knowledge, while Thor, the god of thunder, wields his mighty hammer, Mjölnir, to protect both gods and humans. Loki, the cunning trickster, shifts between ally and adversary, often bringing both chaos and unintended consequences. The myths also foretell Ragnarök, the prophesied end of the world, where gods and monsters clash in a final, cataclysmic battle, leading to both destruction and renewal. These legends, preserved in the Poetic and Prose Eddas, continue to inspire literature, art, and modern storytelling.
